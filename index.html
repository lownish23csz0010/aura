Abstract

Generating appropriate facial reactions from audio-visual features often yields optimal results. However, in many real-world applications, accessing the speakerâ€™s visual information is computationally expensive. To address this limitation, we focus exclusively on au- dio cues. Prior approaches predominantly rely on acoustic features, which capture prosody but fail to incorporate the linguistic content that is crucial for understanding conversational context. To over- come this gap, we introduce AURA, an audio-guided framework that leverages large language models (LLMs) to generate motion tokens representing dynamic sequences of facial expressions, head poses and eye gaze. Our framework not only integrates both acoustic and linguistic cues from speech but also enables the generation of di- verse, contextually appropriate facial reactions.

Approach

Overall Architecture of AURA

Our framework consists of two main components:

  1. Motion Tokenizer - We first process raw motion sequences using a single encoder. The motion is quantized with Finite Scalar Quantization (FSQ), and the resulting tokens are decoded by specialized decoders dedicated to expression, pose, and eye motion. This step creates a compact and structured representation of human motion.
  2. Audio-to-Motion Prediction - Given an input speech signal, we extract its Mel-spectrogram and feed it into a pre-trained audio encoder. To balance efficiency and adaptability, we freeze N blocks and fine-tune M blocks of the encoder. The encoder output is then mapped into the LLM embedding space through an adapter. Finally, a Large Language Model (LLM), fine-tuned with LoRA, predicts the motion tokens that correspond to the speech input.
